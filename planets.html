🪐 Planets

Planets are large celestial bodies that orbit stars. In our solar system, eight major planets travel around the Sun, each with its own environment, size, and atmosphere.

Rocky planets like Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars are called terrestrial planets. The outer giants like Jupiter and Saturn contain large amounts of gas, while Uranus and Neptune are often called ice giants.

Planets can have moons, rings, storms, volcanoes, and even oceans. Earth is special because it is currently the only known planet that supports life.

Beyond our solar system, astronomers have also discovered thousands of exoplanets orbiting distant stars, showing that planets are common across the galaxy.
