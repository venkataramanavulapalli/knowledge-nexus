🕳️ Black Holes

Black holes are among the most extreme objects in the universe. Their gravity is so powerful that even light cannot escape once it passes a boundary called the event horizon.

Most black holes form when very massive stars collapse at the end of their lives. As the core falls inward, matter gets squeezed into an incredibly small region, creating gravity strong enough to trap everything nearby.

Scientists cannot see black holes directly in the usual way, because they emit no light. Instead, they detect them by observing how nearby stars, gas, and light behave around them.

Some black holes are only a few times more massive than the Sun, while supermassive black holes at the centers of galaxies can contain millions or even billions of solar masses.
